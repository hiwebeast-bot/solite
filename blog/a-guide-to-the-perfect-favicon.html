A Guide to the Perfect Favicon in 2025

By The Solite Team · Published on October 29, 2025 · 3 min read

That tiny icon in your browser tab—the favicon—is one of the most overlooked yet crucial elements of your website's identity. It’s your site's digital signature, building brand recognition and trust. But creating a favicon that looks great everywhere is more complex than just uploading a single favicon.ico file. This guide will walk you through everything you need for a perfect favicon setup in 2025.

Why a Complete Favicon Set Matters

A single favicon.ico file is no longer enough. Users access your site from various devices and platforms, each with its own requirements:

  • Desktop Browsers: Display favicons in tabs, bookmarks, and history.
  • Mobile Devices: Use high-resolution "touch icons" for home screen shortcuts (e.g., on iOS and Android).
  • Web App Manifests: PWA (Progressive Web Apps) need icons of various sizes for installation prompts and app listings.
  • Operating Systems: Windows uses special tile icons, and macOS uses them for Safari's "Add to Dock" feature.

A comprehensive set ensures your brand looks sharp and professional everywhere, enhancing user experience and reinforcing your identity.

The Essential Files for a Modern Favicon Package

To cover all your bases, you need a package of several files. Here are the must-haves:

  • favicon.ico: A multi-size file (usually 16x16, 32x32, 48x48) for legacy browser support.
  • favicon-16x16.png and favicon-32x32.png: For modern browsers that prefer PNGs.
  • apple-touch-icon.png: A 180x180px icon for iOS devices.
  • android-chrome-192x192.png and android-chrome-512x512.png: For Android devices and PWA splash screens.
  • site.webmanifest: A JSON file that tells browsers how your web app should behave when 'installed' on a device.

Manually creating all these sizes and files is tedious and error-prone. That's where a good generator comes in.
